Dr. José Rizal

National Hero of the Philippines

The following is the timeline of Dr. Jose Rizal's life:

  • June 19, 1861 - José Rizal is born to become the seventh child born to his parents.
  • June 22, 1861 - Rizal is baptized as Jose Rizal Mercado
  • 1870 - Jose begins school under the instruction of Justiniano Aquin Cruz at just nine years of age.
  • 1872 - Rizal is examined by those in charge of college entrance to St. Tomas University in Manila; he enters the school system as a scholar.
  • March 14, 1877 - Rizal at the age of sixteen obtains his bachelor of arts degree from Ateneo Municipal de Manila with the general average of sobresaliente(excellent).
  • September 11, 1878 - Using the pen name P. Jacinto, Rizal begins his diary Memorias de un estudiante de Manila
  • June 21, 1884 - Rizal is conferred the degree of licentiate in medicine.
  • March 21, 1887 - Copies of Noli Me Tangere become available
  • March 21, 1891 - Rizal finishes his second novel El Filibusterismo, but plans to revise some chapters
  • December 15, 1896 - Imprisoned in Fort Santiago, Rizal writes a manifesto to the Filipinos to stop the revolution. The Spanish authorities, however, did not publish the document.
  • December 30, 1896 - At 7:03 am Rizal is executed. In the afternoon, Narcisa discovers a newly dug grave at Paco Cemetery. She marks it with a marble plaque, with Rizal's initials in reverse.
